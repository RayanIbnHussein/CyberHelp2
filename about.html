What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying involves the use of digital devices such as computers, smartphones, and social media platforms to harass, threaten, or hurt another person. It can take many forms, including sending mean messages, spreading rumors, or sharing someone's private information without consent.

Why is Cyberbullying Harmful?

Cyberbullying can lead to severe emotional distress, including anxiety, depression, and even suicidal thoughts. Unlike traditional bullying, it can happen 24/7, leaving victims feeling vulnerable even in the safety of their own homes.

Our Mission

CyberHelp aims to provide victims with the resources, support, and education they need to combat cyberbullying and promote a safer online environment for everyone.
